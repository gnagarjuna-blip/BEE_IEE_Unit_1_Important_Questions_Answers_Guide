1. State and explain KCL and KVL 5M/10M ▾
Statement (2M):
  • KCL: The algebraic sum of currents meeting at a node is zero. (ΣI = 0)
  • KVL: The algebraic sum of all voltages in a closed loop is zero. (ΣV = 0)
Explanation (5M/10M):

KCL is based on the Law of Conservation of Charge. It states that incoming current equals outgoing current. KVL is based on the Law of Conservation of Energy. In any loop, the sum of EMFs equals the sum of IR drops.

    3. Voltage vs Current Source (Ideal/Practical) 5M/10M ▾

    Ideal Voltage Source: Internal resistance is 0. Voltage remains constant regardless of load.

    Practical Voltage Source: Small internal resistance (Rs) in series.

    Ideal Current Source: Internal resistance is infinite. Current remains constant.

    Practical Current Source: High internal resistance (Rp) in parallel.

    7. Thevenin’s and Norton’s Theorem 5M/10M ▾

    Thevenin's: Any linear network can be replaced by a voltage source Vth in series with Rth.

    Norton's: Any linear network can be replaced by a current source In in parallel with Rn.

    Note: Rn is always equal to Rth.

    8(I). Find V1 and V2 using Nodal Analysis (Green Circuit) 10M ▾
    Node V1: 1 = V1/2 + (V1-V2)/6 ⇒ 4V1 - V2 = 6
    Node V2: (V1-V2)/6 = V2/7 + 4 ⇒ 7V1 - 13V2 = 168
    Solving the system:
    V1 = -2 V
    V2 = -14 V
    8(II). Evaluate current through 10 Ω (Mesh Analysis) 10M ▾
    Mesh 1 (Left): 50 - 10i1 - 5(i1 - i2) = 0 ⇒ 15i1 - 5i2 = 50
    Mesh 2 (Right): -5(i2 - i1) - 10i2 - 25 = 0 ⇒ -5i1 + 15i2 = -25
    Solving:
    i1 = 3.125 A (Current through left 10 Ω)
    i2 = -0.625 A (Current through right 10 Ω)
    Answer: 3.125 A
    10. Three resistors (2Ω, 4Ω, 6Ω) in series across 24V 5M ▾

    Total Resistance (Rt) = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 Ω

    Total Current (I) = V / Rt = 24 / 12 = 2 A

    • Voltage across 2Ω: 2A × 2Ω = 4V
    • Voltage across 4Ω: 2A × 4Ω = 8V
    • Voltage across 6Ω: 2A × 6Ω = 12V

    Total Power = V × I = 24V × 2A = 48 W
