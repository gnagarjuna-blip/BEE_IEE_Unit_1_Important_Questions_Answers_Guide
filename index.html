Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

Statement: The algebraic sum of currents meeting at any junction (node) in an electrical circuit is zero. Sum of incoming currents = Sum of outgoing currents.

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

Statement: In any closed loop of a network, the algebraic sum of all the voltages (potential rises and drops) is zero.

4. Series R-C Circuit Transient (Numerical) 10M

Given: R = 10 Ω, C = 0.1 F, V = 20 V, t = 0.

Equation: i(t) = (V/R) * e^(-t/RC)
RC = 10 * 0.1 = 1 second.
i(t) = 2 * e^(-t) Amperes.
At t=0:
Vr = i(0)*R = 2 * 10 = 20V
Vc = V - Vr = 20 - 20 = 0V (Capacitor acts as a short circuit).

10. Series Resistance Calculation (2, 4, 6 Ω) 5M

Given: R1=2Ω, R2=4Ω, R3=6Ω, V=24V.

  • Total Resistance (Rt): 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 Ω
  • Current (I): V / Rt = 24 / 12 = 2 A
  • Voltage across R1: I * R1 = 2 * 2 = 4 V
  • Voltage across R2: I * R2 = 2 * 4 = 8 V
  • Voltage across R3: I * R3 = 2 * 6 = 12 V
  • Total Power: V * I = 24 * 2 = 48 W
