Unit-1: DC Circuits

Basic Electrical Engineering - Important Questions & Solutions

Part A: Theory Concepts

1. State and explain KCL and KVL (5M/10M)
Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL): The algebraic sum of currents meeting at a junction (node) in an electric circuit is zero.
ΣI = 0. (Incoming currents = Outgoing currents).

Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL): The algebraic sum of all voltages (EMFs and IR drops) in any closed loop of a network is zero.
ΣV = 0.
2. Explain Active and Passive elements? (5M/10M)
Active Elements: Elements capable of delivering or amplifying energy (e.g., Voltage sources, Current sources, Transistors, Op-amps).

Passive Elements: Elements that consume or store energy but cannot generate it (e.g., Resistors, Inductors, Capacitors).
3. Difference between Current and Voltage sources? Ideal vs Practical. (5M/10M)
Voltage Source: Maintains a specific voltage. An Ideal source has zero internal resistance; a Practical source has a small resistance in series.

Current Source: Maintains a specific current. An Ideal source has infinite internal resistance; a Practical source has a high resistance in parallel.
5. Explain V-I characteristics of R, L, C (5M/10M)
Resistor (R): Linear relationship (Ohm's Law: V = IR). The graph is a straight line through the origin.
Inductor (L): V = L(di/dt). Voltage depends on the rate of change of current.
Capacitor (C): I = C(dv/dt). Current depends on the rate of change of voltage.
6. State and explain Superposition Theorem (5M/10M)
Statement: In any linear, bilateral network containing multiple sources, the response (current/voltage) in any branch is the algebraic sum of the responses caused by each source acting alone, while all other independent sources are replaced by their internal resistances.
7. State Thevenin’s and Norton’s Theorems (5M/10M)
Thevenin’s: Any linear circuit can be replaced by a single voltage source (Vth) in series with a resistance (Rth).

Norton’s: Any linear circuit can be replaced by a single current source (In) in parallel with a resistance (Rn).

8(i). Three resistors R1, R2, R3 in series. Find V if current is 5A.

I = 5A, VR1 = 20V, PR2 = 25W, R3 = 2Ω
1. Find R1 using Ohm's Law (R = V/I).
2. Find R2 using Power formula (P = I²R).
3. Find Total Resistance and Total Voltage.
R1 = 20/5 = 4Ω
R2 = P/I² = 25/(5²) = 25/25 = 1Ω
Total R = 4 + 1 + 2 = 7Ω
Total Voltage V = I × R = 5 × 7 = 35 V

9. Transient Response of Series R-L and R-C Circuits

Equation: V = Ri + L(di/dt)
Solution: i(t) = (V/R)[1 - e-(R/L)t]
Time Constant (τ) = L/R. Current grows exponentially.
Equation: V = Ri + (1/C)∫i dt
Solution: vc(t) = V[1 - e-t/RC]
Time Constant (τ) = RC. Voltage across capacitor builds up exponentially.

10. 2Ω, 4Ω, 6Ω in series across 24V. Find V across each.

Total R = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12Ω
Current I = V/R = 24/12 = 2A
V1 (2Ω): 2 × 2 = 4V
V2 (4Ω): 2 × 4 = 8V
V3 (6Ω): 2 × 6 = 12V
Total Power: P = V × I = 24 × 2 = 48W
